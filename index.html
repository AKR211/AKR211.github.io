Hello There. My name is Agney and I am a 4th year Int. MSc. Student @ NISER Bhubaneswar. I'm majoring in Physics (and minoring in Computer Science) with a particular inquisitiveness for Statistical Mechanics, Soft Condensed Matter Physics and Biological Physics. My specific curiosity is in the physics of living matter which lies at the intersection of Physics, Biology and Computational Science. I am interested in understanding complex biological relations and systems such as bird flocks, bacterial colonies, proteins in media etc., creating interaction-based physics models for the same and using computer simulations (aided by machine learning protocols if necessary) to evolve, observe and analyse emergent behaviours. A more detailed explanation of my research interests can be found here.

I picked up programming during my 1st year and my time spent on it has only increased ever since, partly due to my academic interests and also because I very much enjoy writing code. I primarily code in Python but has also experimented with Javascript, Fortran and Julia. I have participated in and won a fair few number of coding and cryptographical challenges with my limited experience. I am always on the look for new and interesting tasks/problems where I can try to put my programming skills to good use and hopefully upgrade my repertoire.

Projects

1st Summer Project: Computational Studies of the Ising Model

  • Date: 2/6/2023 - 31/7/2023
  • Location: NISER, Bhubaneswar
  • Guide: Dr. A. V. Anil Kumar

Explored the phase transition and critical temperature of the 2 dimensional Ising Model for Ferromagnetism using extensive Monte Carlo simulations. Attempted to study changes in critical behaviour with the introduction of non-reciprocal interactions.

W.I.P

Semester Project: Physics Informed Neural Networks

  • Date: 2/6/2023 - 31/7/2023
  • Location: NISER, Bhubaneswar

Explored the concept of Physics Informed Neural Networks(PINNs) as a data-driven approach to solving complex physics equations. Built PINNs from scratch to solve multiple Ordinary Differential Equations using the PyTorch library of Python.

W.I.P

Semester Project: Predicting Protein Oligomericity from Amino Acid Sequence

  • Date: 2/1/2024 - 7/9/2024
  • Location: NISER, Bhubaneswar
  • Guide: Dr. Subhankar Mishra
  • Collaborators: Joel Joseph K. B.

Developed and analyzed machine learning models for the task of predicting the oligomerization state(Monomer, Dimer, Oligomer etc.) of a protein from its primary amino acid sequence. Architectures of the models ranged from CPU-based methods such as k-Nearest Neighbour algorithm to Natural Language Processors(Transformer, Mamba etc.).

W.I.P

2nd Summer Project: Predicting the Stress Tensor for a simulated molecular system

  • Date: 14/5/2024 - 14/7/2024
  • Location: IISER, Kolkata
  • Guide: Dr. Neelanjana Sengupta
  • Collaborators: Pallab Dutta

Attempted to create a GPU-based machine learning algorithm for the prediction of the Cauchy Stress Tensor of a molecular system(such as a protein in a solvent) as an alternative to computationaly expensive molecular dynamics equations. Majority of the efforts were focused on using Graph Neural Networks to predict the atomic stress tensor of a water box as a precursor.

W.I.P
